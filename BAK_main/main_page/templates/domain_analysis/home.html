Domain Analysis

Five Bcl-2 homology domains (BH1–BH4 and transmembrane region)

(Dana Westphal, 2010)

BAK contains 4 Bcl-2 homology domains, including BH1, BH2, BH3 and BH4 (not shown in the diagram).

Domains in Bak protein

  1. BH1 (117 to 136 amino acid positions)

    BH1 and BH2 domains are necessary for apoptosis inhibition, allowing formation of heterodimers with Bax or Bcl-2. (Yin, Oltvai and Korsmeyer, 1994)

  2. BH2 (in 169-184 positions)

    Sequences of BH1 and BH2 regulatory homology domains are conserved among anti and pro-apoptotic members of the Bcl-2 proteins. The homologies are first realized when Bcl-2 is folded into 8 alpha helices, processing all 4 homology domains, including BH4, BH3, BH1 and BH2. (Gurudutta GU1, 2005)

    BH2 The active sites in the conserved domains in the BH1 (glycine, arginine) and BH2 (tryptophan) domains are important as enzymes active site or being a binding regions for a small molecule or a molecule

  3. BH3 (74 to 88 positions)

    BH3 domain is hydrophobic and contains residues required for the pro-apoptotic activity induced by homodimerization, heterodimerization with Bcl-2 and Bcl XL and the initiation of apoptosis. (Miki Hirotani, 1999)

    Binding of the Bak protein relies mostly in the hydrophobic surface groove of the protein.

  4. BH4

    The BH4 domain has an important function in anti-apoptotic function, suppressing cell death. (Miki Hirotani, 1999)

  5. Transmembrane domain

    Transmembrane domains (TMDs) have important functions in protein-protein interactions and in interactions between Bax (Bcl-2 associated X, apoptosis regulator) and prosurvival proeins in the membrane.

    These pro-survival functions of TMDs are promotes specially by homooligomerization and heterooligomerization in bacterial and mitochondrial membranes. (Vicente Andreu-Fernández, 2016)
